About Us: The Tabla Trio

This initiative was started in April 2013 when us 3 started to embark our journey in the field of Tabla. Now these three will be giving their groundbreaking knowledge to promote the art of tabla playing across generations.

Child Tabla Prodigy

Child Tabla Prodigy, despite only starting to play the tabla in 2013, quickly showed that prodigious talent can't be bound by age. Known for captivating audiences with his rapid-fire tabla beats, he has the uncanny ability to produce rhythms that even seasoned tabla players scratch their heads over. This young virtuoso has not only collected awards like they're going out of fashion, but he's also managed to convince his entire school that "tabla time" should be an official school subject. As the youngest member of our trio, he brings a fresh, dynamic energy that is both inspiring and a little bit intimidating.

Baldy Tabla Player

Meet Baldy Tabla Player, a man whose shiny bald head is only outshone by his brilliant playing. Starting his career as a curious child who thought the tabla was a strange wooden drum that could tell stories, Baldy has evolved into a goofy yet profound artist. His unique playing style combines the classical rigor of tabla with whimsical beats that often make you wonder if the tabla is laughing with you. Notorious for wearing colorful turbans to match his mood, Baldy is a fan favorite for his onstage antics and offstage jokes about how his bald head provides better acoustics.

Mature Tabla Artist

Mature Tabla Artist, often called the encyclopedia of tabla, is one of the most in-demand tabla players globally. With a career spanning over four decades, he has accompanied some of the greatest names in music and is revered for his deep and soulful tabla sounds that resonate with the history of Indian classical music. Now stepping into a new role, he is founding a tabla teaching school, aimed at passing on his extensive knowledge to a new generation of players. His school promises to be a blend of rigorous traditional techniques and his own innovative methods, seasoned with stories from his tours around the world where he played from crowded temples to royal palaces.
