New Developments in Tabla

Technology and new generational artists are creating massive shifts impacting to the development of Tabla.

Explore tabla's massive rhytmic and musical scope. Learn how to play this instrument and also explore many different technologies and improvements made throughout history of this instrument.

Tabla is a generation old instrument said to be founded in 1750s by Ustad Shiddhar Khan. It is a percussion instrument that is played by striking the surface of the drum with the fingers. The instrument is made up of two drums, the smaller drum is called the "Dayan" and the larger drum is called the "Bayan". The Dayan is made of wood and the Bayan is made of metal. Recently from the past few years, the tabla has been gaining a lot of popularity and new technologies and new ideas that were considered radical in the past have been diminished and unified. It has now gained a new expression, and is not bound by the rules of hindustani classical music. Currently it follows the indian classical music and its traditions as well as is a great tool for many musicians to express their music, through the beauty of this musical drums. Lets explore the changes tabla has gone through and how it has evolved over the years.
